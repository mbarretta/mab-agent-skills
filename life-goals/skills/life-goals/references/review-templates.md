# Review Templates

Structured formats for each review cadence. The purpose of reviews is not documentation — it's clarity. A good review changes something: your priorities, your habits, your understanding of what's working.

Read the appropriate template for the review type requested.

---

## Weekly Review (~15 minutes)

Best done Sunday evening or Monday morning. This is the keystone habit that keeps everything else on track.

### Template

**WEEKLY REVIEW — Week of [DATE]**

**1. Habit Check (5 min)**
For each active habit in GOALS.md, score the past 7 days:
- ✅ Did it / ❌ Missed it (for daily habits)
- Mark the streak in the goals file

For any missed habit: "What got in the way? One small thing I'll do differently this week:"

**2. Sprint Progress Check (3 min)**
For each sprint goal:
- On track / At risk / Off track?
- One sentence on why

If any goal is at risk or off track: "What's the most important thing I can do this week to get it back on track?"

**3. Last Week's Wins (2 min)**
"What went well last week? What am I proud of?"
(This step is easy to skip — don't. Noticing progress is what sustains motivation.)

**4. This Week's Focus (5 min)**
"The 3 most important things I need to accomplish this week:"
1. [Thing 1 — linked to which goal/sprint]
2. [Thing 2]
3. [Thing 3]

"The single most important one, if I could only do one:"

**5. Energy and Flags**
"How am I doing? Anything that needs attention — personally, relationally, physically?"
"Any upcoming commitments or events that need planning?"

### After the Review

Update `goals/GOALS.md` Weekly Log section. Keep entries brief — this is a running record, not an essay.

## Monthly Review (~30 minutes)

Best done on the last day of the month or first day of the next. More depth than weekly, less scope than quarterly.

### Template

**MONTHLY REVIEW — [MONTH YEAR]**

**1. Habit Trends (5 min)**
Look at the full month of habit tracking:
- Which habits are solid? (consistent streaks)
- Which are struggling? (multiple misses, pattern of slipping)
- One insight: "The habit pattern I'm most noticing is..."

For any struggling habit: Is this a design problem (wrong trigger, wrong time, too hard) or an execution problem? Design problems need a fix. Execution problems need a recommitment.

**2. Sprint Goal Progress (10 min)**
For each sprint goal, what % of the target have you hit by now?

If the sprint ends this month:
- Use the Sprint Retrospective format (see Quarterly Review section)

If the sprint is ongoing:
- Score each goal: On track / At risk / Off track
- "The thing I need to double down on this month:"
- "The thing I need to let go of or simplify:"

**3. Domain Check (5 min)**
Quick scan of all 6 domains: any unexpected changes, wins, or concerns in any domain?

"The domain I'm most neglecting right now:"
"One small thing I could do about it:"

**4. What I Learned (5 min)**
"The most useful thing I've learned or realized this month:"
"Something I want to do differently next month:"

**5. Next Month Preview (5 min)**
"The most important outcome for next month:"
"Anything I need to plan or prepare for?"

### After the Review

Update GOALS.md with monthly progress notes and adjust habit tracking tables as needed.

## Quarterly Review (~60-90 minutes)

This is the full cycle — a sprint ends, a new one begins. The most operationally important review in the system.

### Part 1: Sprint Retrospective (~30 min)

**SPRINT RETROSPECTIVE — [SPRINT DATES]**

**Goal Scoring**
For each sprint goal:
- Final score: X% of target achieved
- On track / Partially achieved / Missed

**Root Cause Analysis**
For each goal that was partially achieved or missed:
- "The primary reason this goal wasn't fully achieved:"
- "Was this a planning problem (unrealistic target, wrong goal) or an execution problem (right goal, didn't follow through)?"

Planning problems → adjust the goal design next sprint
Execution problems → identify the specific habit or behavior to change

**Sprint Wins**
"What am I most proud of from this sprint?"
"What worked well that I want to repeat?"

**Habit Report**
Which habits ran strong this sprint? Which struggled?
"The one habit I want to build on next sprint:"
"The one habit pattern I want to break or redesign:"

**Key Learning**
"The most important thing I learned this sprint about myself, my goals, or what actually works:"

---

### Part 2: Next Sprint Design (~30-45 min)

**SPRINT DESIGN — [NEW SPRINT DATES]**

**Step 1: Read the Annual OKRs and 3-Year Goals**
Before designing the sprint, re-read the full goals context. Sprint goals should meaningfully advance annual OKRs — not just feel productive.

**Step 2: Domain Scan**
Quick 1-10 rescore of all 6 domains:
- Any significant changes from last quarter?
- Any domain that urgently needs attention?

**Step 3: Sprint Theme**
"The theme for this sprint — one phrase that captures its energy and direction:"
(Examples: "Financial Foundation", "Health Reclamation", "Deep Work Season", "Family First")

**Step 4: Sprint Goals**
Define 2-4 sprint goals. For each:
- Goal statement (specific, achievable in 12 weeks)
- Metric: how will progress be measured?
- Target: what's the 12-week finish line?
- Weekly leading indicator: what does being on track look like each week?

The test for a good sprint goal: "If I achieve this, which annual OKR does it advance? By how much?"

**Step 5: Habit Design**
Review active habits — continue, modify, or retire?

For any new sprint goals, design supporting habits using the framework:
- Identity anchor: "I am someone who..."
- Implementation intention: "I will [behavior] at [time] in [location]"
- Habit stack: "After [existing habit], I will [new habit]"
- Metric: how to track it weekly

Cap active habits at 3-5 total. More than that and nothing becomes automatic.

**Step 6: Model Week**
Sketch the ideal week for this sprint:
- When does deep work happen?
- When do key habits execute?
- When does family time occur?
- When does the weekly review happen?

This doesn't need to be detailed — just the anchor commitments that protect what matters.

**Step 7: Write it Down**
Update GOALS.md:
- New sprint section with theme, goals, habits
- Archive the completed sprint
- Update the Domain Overview table

## Annual Review (~2-3 hours)

This is the deepest review — the one worth protecting serious time for. Best done in late December or early January, ideally in a different environment (a coffee shop, a quiet afternoon, anywhere away from normal routines).

The annual review isn't just a bigger quarterly review. It's a chance to zoom out completely and ask: "Is this still the right direction? Am I becoming who I want to become?"

### Part 1: Year in Review (~45 min)

**ANNUAL REVIEW — [YEAR]**

**Accomplishment Inventory**
"List everything significant I accomplished this year across all domains — work, family, health, financial, personal growth. Include things that weren't goals but still matter."

(This step often surprises people — the year was usually better than they felt it was. Taking inventory of real progress before evaluating gaps is important for accurate calibration.)

**OKR Scoring**
For each annual OKR, score each Key Result 0.0-1.0:
- 1.0 = fully achieved
- 0.7 = strong result on an ambitious goal
- Below 0.5 = significant shortfall (requires analysis)

Overall OKR grade: __/__

**Root Cause Analysis** (for any KR below 0.6)
"The primary reason this KR fell short:"
- Wrong goal (shouldn't have been a priority)
- Right goal, poor planning (insufficient habits/projects supporting it)
- Right goal, execution failures (planned but didn't follow through)
- Right goal, external factors (circumstances genuinely changed)

**Domain Assessment**
Re-score all 6 domains 1-10. Compare to last year's scores:
- Which domains improved? Why?
- Which declined or stayed flat? Why?
- Which domains have the highest leverage opportunity in the coming year?

**Life Quality Question**
"In the past year, how often did I feel genuinely alive — engaged, purposeful, doing work that matters and spending time in ways I value?"
"What created those moments? What prevented more of them?"

**The Hard Question**
"Is there anything significant I've been avoiding — about my health, my relationships, my financial situation, my career direction — that I've let another year pass without addressing?"

---

### Part 2: North Star Check (~30 min)

**Does the North Star still hold?**

Re-read the North Star Statement from GOALS.md.

- Does it still ring true, or have your values or vision shifted?
- Has anything in your life changed significantly enough that it needs updating?
- If you were writing it fresh today, would it say the same thing?

If yes, confirm and continue. If partially, refine it. If the vision has substantially changed, treat this as a partial Regenerate (Mode 3) before continuing.

**Life Domain Vision Check**
For each domain's "10 looks like" description:
- Still accurate? Or did your vision of what you want actually change?
- Update any descriptions that feel off.

**10-Year Milestone Check**
You're one year closer. Are the milestones still the right targets? Update dates and specifics as needed.

---

### Part 3: Coming Year Design (~60 min)

**Step 1: Priorities**
"The 1-2 life domains that will be my primary focus this year:"
"The reason these are the priority:"

**Step 2: Annual OKRs**
Design 3-4 annual OKRs for the coming year. Each should:
- Advance a 3-year goal meaningfully
- Be achievable (not easily, but plausibly) in 12 months
- Have 2-3 Key Results that are specific and measurable

**Step 3: 3-Year Check**
Are you on track for each 3-year goal? Do any need to be updated given what you learned this year?

**Step 4: First 90-Day Sprint**
Design the first sprint of the new year using the Quarterly Sprint Design format above.

**Step 5: The One Thing**
"If I had to name the single most important shift — in behavior, in focus, in mindset — that would make this year significantly better than last year, it would be:"

---

### After the Annual Review

Update GOALS.md completely:
- New North Star section if updated
- New Annual OKRs
- Updated 10-year milestones and 3-year goals
- Updated domain table
- New sprint section
- Archive the prior year's goals with brief notes on what happened

Consider: share a summary of your annual goals with someone who matters to you. Accountability doesn't require a formal arrangement — just telling one person what you're building creates meaningful commitment.

## Review Troubleshooting

**"I keep skipping my weekly review."**
The review is too long. Cut it to 5 minutes. Three questions: What's my #1 focus this week? How am I doing on habits? What's one win from last week? Add back complexity only when the 5-minute version is automatic.

**"My goals feel stale and I'm not motivated."**
This usually means the goals aren't connected to what actually matters (they're what you think you should want, not what you actually want) or the North Star is vague. Run a partial Mode 1 (Phase 1 only) to reconnect to values.

**"I keep setting goals and not achieving them."**
Diagnose the failure mode: (a) too many goals — focus on 1-2 at a time; (b) goals without supporting habits — each goal needs a daily/weekly behavior attached; (c) review gaps — without weekly review, execution drifts. Start with the weekly review as the keystone fix.

**"Life changed significantly and my goals feel wrong."**
Use Mode 3 (Regenerate). Don't try to retrofit old goals onto a new reality — that creates guilt without progress. Acknowledge what changed and build a new plan from current ground truth.
