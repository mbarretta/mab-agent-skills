# SE Leader Work Planning

A planning system for a Sales Engineering leader covering the full rhythm of the job: quarterly goal-setting, weekly execution, team development, and market intelligence. Built around the reality that SE leaders are simultaneously managing pipeline, people, and product feedback — all at the same time.

## Persistent State

All work planning data lives at `work/WORK_PLAN.md` in the workspace. This is the living document the system centers on — read it first if it exists.

The skill can also read from an Obsidian vault if a path is configured in `work/CONFIG.md`:
```
obsidian_vault: /path/to/vault
1:1_folder: 1:1s  # subfolder within vault where 1:1 notes live
```
When a vault path exists, the weekly review will pull recent 1:1 notes for context before surfacing coaching priorities.

If no WORK_PLAN.md exists, start with **Mode 1: Quarterly Setup**.

## Modes

| Mode | When to use |
|------|-------------|
| **1. Quarterly Setup** | Start of each quarter — set targets, decompose quota, build team plan |
| **2. Weekly Review** | Every week — pipeline health, team priorities, personal focus, market signals |
| **3. Market Intelligence** | Ad hoc — capture what you're seeing in the field, synthesize for product |
| **4. Quarter Close** | End of quarter — retrospective + feed forward into next quarter |

## Mode 1: Quarterly Setup

Run this at the start of each quarter. Takes 45-90 minutes the first time; faster in subsequent quarters since you're updating rather than building from scratch.

### Step 1: Anchor the Quarter

Gather the fundamentals:
- **Team quota this quarter**: What's the number you're collectively accountable to?
- **Pipeline target**: Pipeline coverage ratio goal (typically 3-4x quota). What's the target pipeline you need entering/building this quarter?
- **Pipeline on hand**: What's currently in the pipe at the start of the quarter? Qualified only — not every early-stage opportunity.
- **Coverage gap**: (Pipeline target) - (Pipeline on hand) = what the team needs to generate this quarter through pipegen activities
- **Key strategic bets**: Are there specific segments, verticals, use cases, or accounts that leadership is prioritizing this quarter?

Read `references/quarterly-planning.md` for pipeline math, coverage ratio guidance, and how to decompose the number into activity targets.

### Step 2: Set the Quarter's Goals

Build 4-6 goals across the three areas of the job. Each goal needs a metric and a target. More than 6 goals means nothing is actually a priority.

**Pipeline & Revenue Goals** (the number)
- Pipeline generated by SEs this quarter (workshops, events, content, AE-assist)
- Pipeline coverage ratio achieved by end of quarter
- # of deals where SE involvement materially advanced the technical evaluation

**Team Development Goals** (the people)
- Enablement coverage: % of team ready to demo/position [key product area or new release]
- Individual development: 1-2 SEs with a specific growth goal this quarter
- Retention/engagement: any at-risk signals to address

**Market & Field Goals** (what you're learning)
- # of customer discovery conversations where you capture structured feedback
- Key competitive encounters to track and respond to
- Top product gap or demo gap to address this quarter (build something)

Read `references/metrics.md` for metric definitions and healthy ranges for each goal type.

### Step 3: Team Roster Update

For each SE on the team, confirm:
- Current focus area / account load
- Development priority this quarter (one thing they're working on growing)
- Any retention flags or personal situations worth tracking
- Planned PTO or absences that affect capacity

### Step 4: This Quarter's Pipegen Calendar

Map out the SE-driven pipeline generation activities planned for the quarter:
- Workshops (customer or prospect-facing): dates, topics, owners
- Conference talks or appearances: confirmed or in submission
- LinkedIn / content: themes, cadence, who's driving
- Internal sales enablement sessions: what and when

Identify gaps: Is the activity volume sufficient to close the coverage gap? If not, what needs to be added or accelerated?

### Step 5: What We Need to Build

Based on what came out of last quarter (or your current field read), identify:
- Demos to build or update
- Workshops to develop or refresh
- Technical content gaps (battle cards, objection handlers, proof points)
- What you need from Product Marketing or Product to be ready for the quarter

### Step 6: Write It Down

Update `work/WORK_PLAN.md` with the quarter's goals, team roster, pipegen calendar, and build list. See the template at the bottom of this file.

## Mode 2: Weekly Review

The heartbeat of execution. Run every week — Monday morning or Friday before the week ends. Should take 20-30 minutes.

### Pull Context First

Before starting the review, pull relevant context:
- Read the current quarter's goals from `work/WORK_PLAN.md`
- If an Obsidian vault path is configured in `work/CONFIG.md`, scan recent 1:1 notes (files modified in the last 7-14 days in the 1:1 folder) to surface coaching moments or follow-ups
- Note any key events this week: customer meetings, pipeline reviews, internal syncs

### Weekly Review Structure

**1. Pipeline Health (5 min)**

For each stage of the pipeline:
- Is coverage where it needs to be for the quarter?
- Any deals that moved forward, stalled, or need SE attention?
- Any technical evaluations at risk?

Key question: "Is there anything in the pipeline right now where SE involvement this week could materially move the deal?"

**2. Pipegen Activity Check (5 min)**

Track against the quarter's pipegen calendar:
- What pipegen activities happened last week? (workshops delivered, content posted, events attended)
- What's coming up this week that needs prep or support?
- Are we on pace to hit the pipeline generation target?

Flag: If pipegen activities are slipping (workshops postponed, content not happening), surface it now rather than in week 10.

**3. Team Coaching Priorities (5 min)**

Surface the 1-2 SEs who most need attention this week:
- Who has a high-stakes customer engagement coming up that needs prep?
- Who has an open development goal that's been quiet?
- Who had something notable in their recent 1:1 that needs follow-up?

If Obsidian notes are available, summarize any relevant threads from recent 1:1s here.

**4. Market Signals (3 min)**

What did you (or the team) see or hear last week that's worth noting?
- New competitive encounter or objection pattern?
- Customer concern or fear that came up repeatedly?
- Product gap that blocked or complicated a deal?

Log anything worth capturing in the Market Intelligence section of `work/WORK_PLAN.md`.

**5. My Top 3 This Week (5 min)**

Given everything above, what are the 3 most important things *you* personally need to do this week?

Not the team's list — yours. As a leader, your highest-leverage activities are usually: closing a coaching loop, unblocking a key deal, or building something the team needs. Make those explicit before the week fills up with reactive work.

End with: "What's the one thing that, if I don't do it this week, I'll regret?"

### After the Review

Update `work/WORK_PLAN.md`:
- Add weekly log entry
- Update pipeline notes if anything changed
- Update any individual SE development notes

## Mode 3: Market Intelligence

Use this mode ad hoc when you've seen something in the field worth capturing — a pattern across customer conversations, a competitive encounter, a product gap that keeps coming up, or a fear/objection you're not ready to address well.

### Capture Structure

For each signal worth logging:

**Signal type**: Competitive / Product gap / Customer fear / Market trend / Objection pattern

**What we're seeing**: 1-3 sentence description. Be specific — "customers are asking about X" is less useful than "3 enterprise prospects in fintech have asked us how we handle Y before they'll consider a POC."

**Frequency/severity**: Is this one-off or a pattern? Is it blocking deals or just a question?

**What we'd need to address it**: Better demo? A whitepaper? A product capability? A reference customer? Something from Product Marketing?

**Who to loop in**: Is this Product, Product Marketing, Field, or something the SE team can handle internally?

Synthesize these into a quarterly "Field Intelligence Report" that you can bring to your Product and Product Marketing counterparts. This is one of the highest-value contributions an SE leader makes — the team is closer to customers than almost anyone else in the company.

Read `references/metrics.md` for what to track in market intelligence and how to present it effectively to Product.

## Mode 4: Quarter Close

Run this in the final week of the quarter or first week of the next. It's a retrospective *and* a setup for what comes next.

### Part 1: Quarter Scorecard

Score each goal from the Quarterly Setup:

| Goal | Target | Actual | Score | Notes |
|------|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| Pipeline generated | $X | $Y | % | |
| Coverage ratio | Xx | Xx | | |
| Enablement coverage | X% | Y% | | |
| [etc.] | | | | |

For any goal below 70% of target:
- Was this a planning problem (wrong target, wrong goal) or an execution problem?
- What specifically got in the way?
- What would we do differently?

### Part 2: Team Assessment

For each SE:
- How did they perform this quarter relative to their development goal?
- What's the one most important thing for them to work on next quarter?
- Any retention signals — positive or negative — to be aware of?

### Part 3: Field Intelligence Summary

Pull together the market signals logged throughout the quarter:
- Top 3 competitive patterns
- Top 3 product gaps
- Top 3 customer fears or objections
- What do we need from Product / Product Marketing next quarter to be better prepared?

This is the artifact to bring to your cross-functional partners.

### Part 4: Feed Forward

What did this quarter teach you that should shape next quarter's planning?
- What activities generated the most pipeline?
- What enablement actually changed behavior vs. what was checkbox compliance?
- What did you build that the team actually used?
- Where did you personally spend time that wasn't worth it?

Then transition into Mode 1 for the new quarter.

## Working Principles

**The SE leader's highest leverage is multiplicative, not additive.** Your job isn't to be the best SE in the room — it's to make every SE on your team better. Time spent coaching a rep through a technical evaluation is often worth more than doing it yourself.

**Pipeline is a leading indicator. Quota attainment is a lagging one.** By the time you know you're going to miss quota, it's too late to fix it. The weekly pipeline review is early warning infrastructure, not a reporting exercise.

**Pipegen activities that SEs own are the most sustainable source of inbound technical interest.** Workshops, conference talks, and technical content build credibility with buyers before they're in an active sales cycle. They take time to produce results, which is exactly why they need to be planned and tracked early in the quarter rather than scrambled for in week 8.

**Market intelligence is the SE team's gift to the product org.** SEs hear things in customer conversations that PMs and PMMs rarely hear directly — real objections, real fears, real competitive moments. Capturing and synthesizing this systematically makes your team valuable beyond quota and makes you a better cross-functional partner.

**1:1s are the most important meeting on your calendar.** Individual development, retention signals, and coaching depth all flow through 1:1s. Tracking what matters across them is what separates reactive management from intentional leadership.
